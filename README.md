# Planetary Coordination Framework

> A working draft of a post-capitalist economic and governance framework, built in the open so that something coherent is lying around when the current system's failures force a reckoning.

## What this is

This repository is an attempt to sketch — in enough detail to be criticized, forked, and improved — a global economic and governance framework designed for three conditions the current system does not handle:

1. **Abundance is technically possible.** With current and near-term AI, robotics, solar, and logistics, meeting basic human needs for everyone on Earth within planetary boundaries is an engineering problem, not a resource problem.
2. **Coordination is the actual bottleneck.** Free-market price signals fail at planetary scale because most relevant costs — climate, biodiversity, demand destruction from automation, attention extraction, epistemic pollution — are unpriced externalities. Markets cannot self-correct against forces they cannot see.
3. **The population that would democratically choose an alternative has been systematically weakened.** Sick bodies, fractured attention, atomized communities, captured information environments. "Just vote harder" is not a plan. Any serious framework has to account for a population that is not, at the moment, capable of the deliberation required to design its own replacement institutions.

This is not a manifesto and not a finished theory. It is a skeleton. It is being published now, in public, because the next crisis will pick up whichever coherent alternative is lying around — and right now, nothing coherent is on the shelf.

## Core shape

The framework has **three layers**:

**Layer 1 — Planetary constraints.** A small set of non-negotiable hard limits: carbon budget, biodiversity floor, freshwater, topsoil, ocean acidification, and a livelihood floor for every human (food, shelter, healthcare, education, dignity). These are constitutional. No layer below can violate them. They are set by transparent scientific bodies using open methodology, not by political negotiation.

**Layer 2 — Coordination.** A federated set of transparent, open-stack AI systems that handle the allocation and logistics problems that markets handle badly at planetary scale: climate, pandemics, critical supply chains, externality pricing, the planetary dividend, AI governance itself. The AI layer operates on a **public, versioned, amendable instruction set** that anyone can read. It **proposes actions by default; humans override with justification.** Multiple independent systems cross-audit each other. The entire stack — weights, data, logs, hardware provenance — is open.

**Layer 3 — Free middle.** Below the constraints and the coordinator, human life runs without central planning: local economies, culture, small and medium enterprise, experimentation, community governance, religion, art, weirdness. This is where most people actually live, and it is deliberately plural and decentralized. Markets operate here — but as bounded markets, inside a structure that prevents them from eating the commons above them.

The coordinator is explicitly **thin**. It handles what markets and local governance provably cannot. Everything else is left free, and this is a constitutional commitment, not a convenience.

## Why AI

The AI coordination layer answers a problem every post-market framework has had to solve and most have solved badly: **who coordinates when you don't trust markets to do it?**

Historical answers have been central committees (Soviet), technocratic planners (Cybersyn, European technocracy), or assumed-benevolent vanguards (every revolutionary tradition). All of these concentrate power in humans who become the new elite.

This framework's answer: **a transparent, open-stack AI coordinator whose objective function is a public document anyone can read.** Not because AI is wise, but because it is *inspectable* in a way humans are not. You don't have to trust the UN, the US, China, or any corporation. You read the instruction set. Disagreement about outcomes becomes disagreement about the instruction set, which is a much more tractable argument.

The coordinator's authority is **time-limited and domain-limited**. It exists to hold the planetary commons stable during the transition period while the social fabric heals enough for humans to resume self-governance. A **sunset clause** is built in. This is the *regency* framing: the coordinator plays the role a regent plays for a sovereign not yet fit to rule. It is not the end state.

## What this is not

- **Not a replacement for states.** Proposed as a parallel coordinating layer for planetary commons that states have already failed to manage. Grows from there.
- **Not a world government.** No parliament, no executive, no sovereignty over domestic policy.
- **Not Soviet planning.** The coordinator is deliberately thin. Local economies, personal choices, cultural life, and most business activity are explicitly out of its scope.
- **Not anti-market.** Markets work well at small and medium scale. The framework preserves them in the free middle layer, while preventing them from externalizing costs onto the planetary commons above.
- **Not utopia.** The [failure modes](FAILURE_MODES.md) are named explicitly. Several are unsolved.
- **Not finished.** The [open questions](OPEN_QUESTIONS.md) are the point. This document is a starting condition for collaborative work, not a conclusion.
