# Open Questions

The framework is a skeleton. These are the joints where the bones need to be connected, shaped, or replaced. Each question below requires deliberation by people with specialist knowledge. Contributors with relevant expertise are especially welcome on these.

The questions are grouped by layer and ordered roughly by how load-bearing they are. Resolving the earlier ones changes the downstream ones. Contributors should expect iteration.

## Foundational questions

### 1. What is the exact list of planetary constraints (Tier 1)?

Current candidates are the nine planetary boundaries (Rockström et al.), the livelihood floor, and a small set of human rights. But:

- How is each constraint operationalized? (Carbon as tCO2e/year budget? As concentration? As a temperature-indexed dynamic limit?)
- What are the quantitative thresholds? By what methodology? Revised how often?
- Which human conditions count as "livelihood floor"? What standard — global minimum, regional adjustment, absolute or relative?
- How are conflicts between constraints resolved when they arise?
- Are cultural and spiritual continuity protections part of Tier 1, or are they part of Tier 2 objectives?

### 2. Who determines the constraints?

"Scientific bodies with transparent methodology" is the principle, but:

- Which specific bodies? Existing (IPCC, IPBES) or new?
- How are they constituted? Selection, funding, governance of the scientific bodies themselves?
- How is scientific disagreement handled? What's the process when expert opinion is divided?
- How do non-Western scientific and knowledge traditions participate? This is a serious open question — "science" in practice has been heavily shaped by Western institutional structures, and a planetary framework needs more pluralism than current international scientific bodies have demonstrated.
- What happens when science updates in ways politically unpopular with the coordinator's human oversight bodies?

### 3. How are conflicts between Tier 1 constraints resolved?

Carbon budget might require trade-offs against biodiversity (e.g., large-scale biomass energy, lithium mining for batteries). Livelihood floor in the short term may require resource use that strains ecological constraints. Who decides, and by what process?

## Governance questions

### 4. What is the exact structure of override bodies?

Sortition, rotation, and multiple concurring bodies are the principles. Specifics needed:

- How are assembly members selected? From what pool? With what duration of service?
- How large should assemblies be? Research suggests ~100-300 for deliberation; confirm and specify.
- What information and expertise support do members receive? How is that support itself structured to avoid capture?
- How many bodies must concur for what levels of override? Simple decisions vs. major overrides vs. Tier 1 amendments?
- How are members protected from external pressure, bribery, or coercion?
- What's the geographic/cultural distribution? Planetary body vs. regional bodies vs. both?
- How are members compensated? Enough to make service attractive without creating a permanent class of "professional sortition participants"?

### 5. How is the stack capture problem solved?

This is the framework's hardest unsolved problem. The AI coordinator's legitimacy depends on the full stack being open and auditable. But:

- Who manufactures the compute? Currently concentrated in very few entities (TSMC, Samsung, SK Hynix, Nvidia). Planetary-scale open compute is a massive industrial undertaking.
- Who trains the models? Frontier training currently requires capital and talent concentrated in a handful of organizations. Open-weight training at frontier scale is not yet demonstrated.
- How is training data curated and audited? Data quality and provenance at planetary scale is a hard problem.
- How are models updated? Continuous updates introduce capture opportunities; frozen models become stale.
- How is hardware integrity verified? Side-channel attacks, backdoors, and supply-chain compromises are real.
- What's the transition path from current (completely captured) AI infrastructure to the required (fully open) infrastructure?

This may require something like a CERN-scale international public AI project. Or multiple such projects for federation. Or a combination of state-sponsored and cooperative approaches. Specialist proposals welcome.

### 6. What are the amendment process mechanics?

The instruction set is amendable, with different tiers requiring different processes. Specifics:

- Who can propose amendments? Any human? Only contributing organizations? Only citizen assemblies?
- What's the comment and deliberation period for each tier?
- What level of consensus is required? Supermajority? Qualified consensus? Something else?
- How are amendments protected from rushed consideration during crises?
- How are amendments protected from indefinite blockage by minority factions?
- How is the amendment process itself amended?

### 7. How does the interpretation layer work in practice?

- Who sits on interpretation bodies? How selected?
- What's the relationship between AI-assisted drafting of interpretations and human final decision?
- How are contradictory interpretations reconciled? Appeals? Unification?
- How does interpretation relate to amendment? When is the right response "interpret the clause" vs. "rewrite the clause"?
- Does the interpretation body have authority over the coordinator's readings of the instruction set, or does the coordinator operate on the text plus accumulated interpretations natively?

## Economic questions

### 8. What is the level of the livelihood floor?

"Dignified life anywhere on Earth" is the principle. Specifics:

- Absolute level in terms of goods and services guaranteed?
- How is "dignified" defined — and by whom? What's the process for determining the floor in different cultural contexts without imposing a single standard?
- How is the floor indexed (local cost of living, absolute global level, hybrid)?
- How is it adjusted over time as productive capacity grows?
- What's the relationship between floor provision (decommodified goods) and dividend payment (monetary)?

### 9. How is the planetary fund actually capitalized?

Externality pricing, AI productivity capture, land value, and extraordinary profits are the identified sources. Questions:

- Specific rates for each externality? How are they calibrated to Pigouvian optima given measurement uncertainty?
- Treatment of existing fossil fuel reserves, ecosystem destruction, etc. — is there a one-time wealth transfer from existing holders of "externality-intensive" assets?
- How is AI productivity capture implemented? Tax on AI firms? Ownership stake in frontier models? Mandatory licensing?
- Is there monetary creation by the fund, or only redistribution of collected revenue?
- How is the fund governed? (The dividend is simple — equal share to every human. The fund itself has complex allocation and investment questions.)

### 10. What are the firm size thresholds?

Small / chartered / utility is the principle. Specifics:

- Revenue, employment, market share, asset base, or what combination for each threshold?
- How do the thresholds scale with sector? (A "large" restaurant is very different from a "large" energy producer.)
- How is gaming of thresholds prevented? (Shell companies, related-party transactions, sector redefinition.)
- What's the transition process for firms crossing thresholds?
- International firms: which jurisdiction's thresholds apply?

### 11. How does the wealth-tax regime actually work?

- Exact rates and thresholds for wealth tax, estate tax, capital gains?
- Valuation methodology for illiquid assets? (Historically a major gaming vector.)
- Exit taxation and anti-avoidance?
- Treatment of collectively held wealth (family foundations, trusts, cooperative ownership)?
- Transition period for existing concentrated wealth?
- How is enforcement achieved across jurisdictions before the full Layer 2 coordination is in place?

### 12. Monetary systems — national, planetary, or plural?

The framework is silent on whether national currencies continue, whether a planetary unit of account exists, whether multiple currencies coexist. This is load-bearing for how the dividend is paid, how externality prices are denominated, how cross-border flows are handled.

### 13. How are pensions, existing social insurance, and sovereign debt handled in transition?

Most developed-world populations have existing pension entitlements, social insurance claims, and participate in economies structured around sovereign debt. The framework's transition must integrate with these or face massive opposition from affected populations (who include, crucially, aging voters in democracies).

## Transition questions

### 14. Adoption sequencing — which components first?

The framework can be adopted in pieces. Which pieces should be prioritized? Specifically:

- Is the coordinator for climate the first entry point? AI governance? Pandemic response? Ocean governance?
- What are the minimal viable versions of each component?
- Which components can be adopted sub-globally (a coalition of jurisdictions) vs. which require genuine planetary adoption to function?
- What's the sequencing strategy that produces the best early wins and builds coalition?

### 15. How are first-mover jurisdictions protected?

Jurisdictions that adopt components first may face economic retaliation, capital flight, or political pressure. Some form of coalitional protection is needed. Concrete proposals:

- Mutual aid among adopting jurisdictions?
- Tariff and capital-control tools?
- Coalition-of-the-willing structures similar to Climate Club proposals?

### 16. How does the framework relate to existing multilateral institutions?

UN, World Bank, IMF, WTO, WHO, IPCC, and others are the existing planetary governance infrastructure. The framework could:

- Replace them entirely (unlikely viable)
- Absorb their functions into the coordinator
- Operate parallel to them, gradually superseding
- Reform them from within
- Use them as hosts for framework components while their structures are renovated

A clear strategy is needed, and it probably varies by institution.

### 17. How does the framework handle specifically authoritarian adoption?

If China, Russia, or another authoritarian regime adopts framework components without the accompanying governance commitments (open instruction sets, override bodies, sunset clauses, etc.), what's the response? The framework's legitimacy depends on the full stack; selective adoption produces a very different thing.

## Cultural and political questions

### 18. How does the framework handle religious, cultural, and national identity concerns?

Planetary coordination can be perceived as (and can become) flattening of local identity. The framework's Layer 3 freedom is meant to protect against this, but the perception battle matters:

- How is the framework described in contexts where sovereignty and identity concerns are central?
- What accommodations exist for cultural and religious traditions that resist aspects of the framework?
- How are Indigenous governance traditions integrated rather than overridden?

### 19. What's the communication strategy?

The framework name and meme-level presentation is unresolved. Candidates exist but no consensus has emerged. This is partly intentional (naming-by-committee on day one is usually bad) but cannot remain indefinite.

### 20. How is the framework defended against co-optation?

Powerful actors adopt emancipatory language while gutting substance. This will happen. Structural protections:

- Clear, specific commitments that substance requires (open stack, sunset clauses, override bodies with specific structure)
- An evaluation framework: something is "framework-consistent" only if it includes these specific features
- A community willing to name violations publicly

## Meta-questions

### 21. Is the framework itself missing entire categories of concern?

The author of the initial draft is a North American developer. The framework will be missing important concerns that are more visible from other standpoints. Specific invitations:

- Critiques from Global South perspectives on whether "planetary coordination" reproduces colonial dynamics
- Critiques from Indigenous scholars on whether the framework adequately integrates non-Western governance traditions
- Critiques from feminist political economy on whether care work and reproductive labor are adequately centered
- Critiques from disability justice on whether the framework's "dignified life" baseline is genuinely universal
- Critiques from ecological economics on whether the framework is still too anthropocentric
- Critiques from religious traditions on spiritual dimensions the framework ignores

All of these are anticipated to surface significant gaps. The framework treats them as contributions, not threats.

### 22. What are the framework's deal-breakers?

Some modifications would change the framework into something fundamentally different. Tentatively:

- **Removing the sunset clause** would turn this into permanent AI governance. Deal-breaker.
- **Closing the stack** would turn this into captured AI governance. Deal-breaker.
- **Eliminating Layer 3 freedom** would turn this into Soviet-style total planning. Deal-breaker.
- **Removing override authority** would turn this into unaccountable AI rule. Deal-breaker.
- **Removing wealth-concentration limits** would allow the pre-framework power structure to reassert itself. Deal-breaker.

What else belongs on this list? What are the identity-defining commitments that must not be traded away in any modification?

### 23. What are the framework's wrong answers?

Not failure modes (which are in [FAILURE_MODES.md](FAILURE_MODES.md)) but specific claims the framework makes that may simply be wrong:

- Is the demand externality analysis actually as general as we're treating it?
- Is "regency" the right framing, or is it patronizing in a way that undermines legitimacy?
- Is the three-layer structure actually three layers, or are there more required?
- Is subsidiarity the right coordinating principle, or does it hide important failures?

Skeptical contributions welcome.
